4. Data Cleaning

  Data cleaning is an essential process in data analysis that involves identifying and correcting errors, inconsistencies, and inaccuracies in a dataset. Excel is a widely used software for data cleaning due to its ease of use and flexibility.

4.1 Removing Duplicates Record

1. Select the range of cells that you want to check for duplicates.
2. Click on the "Data" tab in the ribbon at the top of the Excel window.
3. Click on the "Remove Duplicates" button in the "Data Tools" group.
4. In the "Remove Duplicates" dialog box, select the columns that you want to use for identifying duplicates. By default, all columns are selected.
5. Click on the "OK" button to remove the duplicate records.

Excel will display a message box indicating how many duplicate records were removed. Click on the "OK" button to close the message box.

4.2 Verifying Distinct Values

1. Select the range of cells that you want to check for distinct values. This can be a single column or multiple columns.
2. Click on the "Data" tab in the ribbon at the top of the Excel window.
3. Click on the "Sort & Filter" button in the "Sort & Filter" group.
4. In the drop-down menu, click on "Advanced" to open the "Advanced Filter" dialog box.
5. In the "Advanced Filter" dialog box, choose "Copy to another location" option under "Action".
6. In the "List range" field, enter the range of cells that you selected in step 2.
7. In the "Copy to" field, specify a destination range where you want to paste the distinct values. Make sure the destination range has enough empty cells to accommodate the distinct values.
8. Check the box next to "Unique records only" to filter for distinct values.
9. Click on the "OK" button to apply the advanced filter and paste the distinct values to the specified destination range.

Excel will copy only the distinct values to the destination range you specified, and you can now verify the distinct values in that range.

6.0 Conclusion and Recommendation

  Based on the findings presented in the article, it can be concluded that the majority of users are actively using the smart device for fitness tracking, but some users are unable to complete the full 31-day period. The data also suggests that users are more active during the weekdays and least active on Sundays. The users' wearing time and sleep patterns also vary throughout the week.

  To improve the usage and completion rates of the smart device, it is recommended to further investigate why some users are unable to complete the full 31-day period and address any issues that may arise. It may also be helpful to provide additional support or resources to encourage users to continue using the device. Additionally, understanding the users' demographics could provide insights into how to tailor the device to better suit their needs. Finally, providing personalized feedback and recommendations based on the users' activity and sleep patterns could help motivate them to continue using the smart device.
